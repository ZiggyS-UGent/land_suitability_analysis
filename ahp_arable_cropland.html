Question 1: In your professional opinion, what characterises land with optimal conditions for arable cropland in Eastern Denmark without relying on socio-economic factors such as the use of fertilisers and pesticides, irrigation and regulatory or economic policy instruments?
In this section, you will evaluate the relative importance of different geo-ecological variables in achieving optimal land conditions for arable cropland.

The variables represent key aspects of soil, landform, hydrology, and climate:

- Soil clay content (%): The proportion of mineral particles smaller than 0.002 mm in the soil.
- Soil organic content (%): The proportion of organic matter in the soil.
- Slope (°): The angular inclination of the land surface relative to the horizontal plane.
- Soil moisture (%): A relative measure of the water content of the soil derived from the Topographic Wetness Index.
- Solar exposure (%): A relative measure of the total incoming solar energy at the land surface.
